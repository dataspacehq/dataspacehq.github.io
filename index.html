Questions

The things people ask first.

Isn't this just an API in front of Snowflake?

No. An API is one of several output ports. DataSpace is a factory for complete, governed data domains — with a lifecycle (ingest → transform → serve), enforced company guardrails, and a fleet console across all of them. The endpoint is the easy part; the governance and consistency are the product.

Does my data leave Snowflake?

Never. DataSpace is a Native App that runs entirely inside your Snowflake account. Nothing is copied to us or anywhere else. You pay Snowflake for compute directly; we're a software layer on top.

Do we still need Snowpipe, dbt, SPCS, RBAC?

DataSpace uses them under the hood — you just don't wire them. Transforms are dbt (generated from your blueprint), serving runs on Snowpark Container Services, governance maps to Snowflake roles and policies. Best practices, by default.

What about data that lives in Databricks or another lakehouse?

On the roadmap: govern and serve data in an external Iceberg catalog (Databricks, Glue) through Snowflake — without moving it. If that's your setup, tell us — we're picking design partners for exactly this.

Is it production-ready today?

We're in early access and onboarding a small group of design partners now — hands-on, shaping the roadmap with us. If you're scaling on Snowflake and feeling the sprawl, this is the moment to get in.
